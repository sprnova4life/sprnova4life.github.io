Designing to the Edges: UDL 3.0 at a Glance

Building on the terminology from Act 5.01, this organizer maps CAST's nine guidelines across the three principles of UDL, with a note under each guideline connecting it to what that support already looks like in my classroom.

The Goal of UDL Learner agency: purposeful & reflective, resourceful & authentic, strategic & action-oriented
Principle · The Why

Engagement

Guidelines 7–9

7 · Welcoming Interests & Identities

Choice, relevance, joy, and fewer needless threats or distractions.

In my classroomIndividualized, self-paced work already builds in choice of pace and sequence for every student across both houses.
8 · Sustaining Effort & Persistence

Clear goals, matched challenge, real collaboration, belonging, feedback that moves work forward.

In my classroomExtension work for a student like C who has met the objective and needs the ceiling raised.
9 · Emotional Capacity

Self-awareness, reflection, and empathy built into how the class runs.

In my classroomOne-on-one re-engagement when I catch a student disengaging while circulating, rather than waiting for it to surface as a behavior issue.
Principle · The How of Input

Representation

Guidelines 1–3

1 · Perception

More than one way to physically take in the same information.

In my classroomPreferential seating and graphic organizers for a student with dyscalculia, alongside a calculator for the computation itself.
2 · Language & Symbols

Clarifying vocabulary and notation, honoring more than one language.

In my classroomConcrete counters, yellow and purple jelly beans, before introducing the abstract symbols of integer addition.
3 · Building Knowledge

Connecting new content to what a student already carries in with them.

In my classroomRegrounding a student in the zero-pair concept with counters before introducing a number line as a second model of the same idea.
Principle · The How of Output

Action & Expression

Guidelines 4–6

4 · Interaction

More than one way to respond, navigate, and move through a task.

In my classroomExtended time built into a student's IEP so the pace of a task doesn't stand in for the pace of their thinking.
5 · Expression & Communication

Multiple media and tools available for producing the finished work.

In my classroomA student can show a solved problem with counters, a drawn number line, or written notation, whichever demonstrates the thinking most clearly.
6 · Strategy Development

Goal setting, planning ahead for obstacles, monitoring one's own progress.

In my classroomChecking in with a newly arrived student on where they are academically before assuming what level of independence they can manage.
Connecting to Act 5.01 Terminology

UDL is proactive. Accommodations and modifications are individual.

UDL is how a lesson is built from the start so it already has room for the range of learners in front of me. Accommodations change how a student accesses that lesson, such as extended time or a calculator, without changing what they're expected to know. Modifications change the expectation itself. Under IDEA, the goal of the Least Restrictive Environment is to keep a student in the setting where accommodations, not modifications, are enough to reach the same standard as their peers.
