Universal Design for Learning, the Anchor Competencies Framework, and culturally sustaining pedagogy usually get taught as three separate frameworks. Inside an actual classroom, they lean on each other constantly.

Real access needs all three

A lesson can offer every option UDL calls for and still fall flat without a student's trust, or without options that reflect who that student actually is. The three frameworks answer three different questions about the same classroom, and a lesson only fully works when all three answers are in place.
